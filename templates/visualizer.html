visualizer.fm

In collaboration with a friend, Jason Sundram, I built a music visualizer to let people explore music in new ways and test some of the capabilities of HTML5. Our visualizer can search for audio, pull the associated analysis from The Echo Nest and play back the audio while simultaneously displaying a unique visualization based on the analysis. Absolutely everything is done in javascript using the canvas and audio elements. (although we do fall back to flash for audio playback in firefox)

The included visualizations look at different features of the audio including tempo, pitch, loudness, and timbre across multiple segments. It turns out that it's very difficult to make a visualization that "feels right" in sync with audio unless you make heavy use of very obvious features like loudness. This is probably why 99% of all visualizers don't go deeper than an FFT.

Check the video above, or you can go try it yourself at visualizer.fm
