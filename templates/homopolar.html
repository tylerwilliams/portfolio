A homopolar motor kit I made as a christmas present for some of my engineering-type friends.

Here's how they work:

  • - Current flows down through the screw, *radially* outward through the magnet, and back up the wire into the battery.
  • - The magnetic field from the magnet is vertically aligned through the magnet's axis of symmetry.
  • - The charges running through the wire (current) feel a force perpendicular to both their direction and the direction of the magnetic field, ie, a tangential force. (this is called a Lorentz force) This torque makes the screw and magnet spin.
