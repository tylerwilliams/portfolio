Stringer

There's been a kinect kicking around The Echo Nest office since they came out, and I've been dying for a chance to use it. At Music Hack Day New York I finally got a chance. I worked with Alex Chen and Aidan Feldman to create an invisible string instrument over the weekend.

Alex had ported the strings from his mta.me demo to Processing, so we first interfaced a kinect using Daniel Shiffmanâ€™s kinect library. This worked really well for a proof of concept, but we wanted some better hand-tracking for our demo.

I setup OpenNI and NITE and got the PointTracker sample running with multiple hands. Everything went swimmingly, so I integrated an OSC library with the PointTracker to send OSC messages to Processing. (thanks Jason for the c++ help!) Every time a new hand is detected, a message is sent to Processing with a new hand ID, and from then on its coordinates as it moves are also sent.

You can see a little bit of "stickiness" in the above video where strings are getting stuck in a plucked state and not vibrating. This is a bug we later worked out. Here's another video showing the fixed version with dynamic strings.
